Bergen County

Bergen County showcases diverse ecosystems including woodlands, various waterways, lakes, and a small mountainous region, providing habitats to wildlife and plants.

Bergen County's enviroment and ecosystems have common characteristics with Hudson County, Essex County, and Passaic County.

Climate

Bergen County has a Humid Subtropical Climate. With temperatures ranging from about 86°F in July to about 19°F in january. Nor'eastors, which are strong winds from the north east, are common during the winter and fall and can bring heavy rain and very strong winds.
The Atlantic Hurricane season typically runs from June 1st to November 30th. However Hurricanes are infrequent in New Jersey as only 15 hurricanes have passed New Jersey since 1850 and few have made landfall. Usually turning into tropical storms before landfall. However, this can be devasting for the coast due to potential Storm Surges that can form from the winds moving cyclonically around a storm. Storm surges cause water to overflow onto coastal lands and cause flooding in the area and can result in massive damage, injuries and even deaths.

Wildlife

Bergen County provides a diverse selection of habitats to wildlife along various rivers and lakes as well as in expansive woodlands .

Mammals

Bergen County houses various mammals well-suited to its suburban and natural environments. Common species include chipmunks, red foxes, and white-tailed deer. The mink may be found near water bodies, while raccoons adapt to urban and suburban settings. Bobcats occasionally appear in more secluded areas close to the mountains.

Big Brown Bat, Woodland Jumping Mouse, Groundhog, White-tailed Deer

Birds

Bergen County hosts a variety of birds adapted to its diverse landscapes. The American robin, Northern cardinal, and red-tailed hawk are common in suburban and wooded areas. Sparrows, like the Song Sparrow, frequent residential spaces, while water bodies attract waterfowl such as mallards and great blue herons. Raptors like the Cooper's hawk soar overhead, contributing to the county's avian diversity.

Aquatic and Marine Life

In the waterways of Bergen County, a diversity of aquatic life contributes to the local ecosystems. Freshwater environments host fish species like bluegill and largemouth bass, while water bodies also support various aquatic invertebrates and contribute to the overall biodiversity.

Insects and Arachnids

Bergen County is home to a variety of insects and arachnids, contributing to its ecological diversity. Butterflies like the Eastern tiger swallowtail and dragonflies are common in various habitats. Arachnids such as orb-weaving spiders and jumping spiders weave intricate webs in wooded areas.

Amphibians and Reptiles

Bergen County hosts a variety of amphibians and reptiles that contribute to the region's biodiversity. Bodies of water areas may harbor amphibians such as the American toad and painted turtle, while woodlands provide habitats for reptiles like the Eastern garter snake and Eastern box turtle. These diverse creatures play essential roles in local ecosystems, highlighting the ecological richness of Bergen County.

Plants and Fungi

Flora

In Bergen County, diverse flora, including oak and maple trees, goldenrod, black-eyed Susan, dogwood, and viburnum shrubs, enrich the landscapes. These plant species collectively contribute to the ecological balance of the county.

Fungi

Bergen County's ecosystems host various fungi, playing essential roles in nutrient cycling. Examples include the Amanita muscaria and Lion's Mane mushrooms along with Shelf fungi, like the artist's conk, contribute to decomposition, recycling nutrients back into the soil.

Enviroments

Rivers, Creeks and lakes

Bergen County is traversed by a network of rivers, creeks, and lakes, contributing to its scenic beauty and ecological diversity. The Hackensack River and its tributaries, such as Pascack Brook, traverse the county, providing habitats for aquatic life. Lakes like Lake Tappan and Woodcliff Lake provide habitats to organisms and recreation to residents.

Woodlands

Bergen County's woodlands, including areas like the Palisades Interstate Park and Ramapo Valley County Reservation, showcase a variety of trees such as the towering oak and the resilient Eastern hemlock. The forest floor hosts native plants like Jack-in-the-Pulpit, as well as provide a habitat to wildlife, contributing to the region's biodiversity.

Picture is of Alpine Woodlands

Mountains

Bergen County, while not having very high mountains has moutain like enviroments in the region. The county's elevated terrains and hilly landscapes create a diverse environment. Wooded hillsides, like those found in the Ramapo Mountains, showcase a mix of deciduous and coniferous trees. The landscape provides scenic overlooks, contributing to the natural beauty of the region. These areas may include hiking trails, providing opportunities for outdoor recreation amid the county's elevated and picturesque environment.
