Climate

Cape May County has a Humid Subtropical Climate. With temperatures ranging from about 85°F in July to about 42°F in january. Cape May County has windy conditions throughout the year. Nor'eastors, which are strong winds from the north east, are common during the winter and fall and can bring heavy rain, very strong winds, flooding and damage.
The Atlantic Hurricane season typically runs from June 1st to November 30th. However Hurricanes are infrequent in New Jersey as only 15 hurricanes have passed New Jersey since 1850 and few have made landfall. Usually turning into tropical storms before landfall. However, can be devasting for the coast due to potential Storm Surges that can form from the winds moving cyclonically around a storm. Storm surges cause water to overflow onto coastal lands and cause flooding in the area and can result in massive damage, injuries and even deaths.

Wildlife

Cape May County's mix of both coastal and inland habitats make the county a home for a variety of wildlife.

Mammals

Cape May County harbors a range of terrestrial mammals within its diverse landscapes. Common mammals in the region include white-tailed deer, eastern gray squirrels, raccoons, and opossums. Woodlands and suburban areas support a variety of small mammals, contributing to the biodiversity of South Jersey's ecosystems.

This section excludes marine mammals which can be found in the aquatic and marine life section.

Pictures from left to right starting at top left
Common Raccoon, Southern Flying squirrel, Woodland Vole, Coyote

Birds

Cape May County is a haven for birdwatchers, offering a rich diversity of avian life. Common sightings include migratory birds such as warblers, hawks, and waterfowl. The region's woodlands, marshes, and coastal environments provide habitats for resident and migratory species, making Cape May County a prime destination for bird enthusiasts throughout the year.

Pictures from left to right starting at top left
Great Black Backed Gull, Great Blue Heron, Northern Cardinal, Sanderling

Aquatic and Marine Life

Common aquatic life in Cape May County includes bottlenose dolphins, pinfish, flounder, and various species of crabs. Estuaries support the presence of diverse fish and invertebrates, creating a dynamic ecosystem that influences the broader marine life of South Jersey.

Pictures from left to right starting at top left
Sandbar Shark, Atlantic Ghost Crab, Pinfish, Atlantic Harbour Porpoise

Insects and Arachnids

Common sightings of insects and arachnids in Cape May County encompass butterflies, dragonflies, and bees, contributing to the region's pollination dynamics. Woodlands, meadows, and coastal areas attract a variety of spiders, beetles, ants and more.

Pictures from left to right starting at top left
Carolina Mantis, Ailanthus Webworm Moth, Eastern Pondhawk, Putnam's Jumping Spider

Amphibians and Reptiles

Cape May County's woodlands, wetlands, and suburban areas are home to many different frogs, toads, salamanders, newts, snakes, tutrles, and lizards. Common amphibians and reptiles include marbled salamaders, american bullfrogs, sea turtles, various snakes and more.

Plants and Fungi

Flora

Cape May County's flora is characterized by coastal beach grasses, seaside goldenrod, inland oak and pine woodlands, and wetland vegetation like cattails. This diverse array of plant life contributes to the county's ecological richness

Fungi

Cape May County hosts a variety of fungi, including mushrooms and molds. These organisms play a vital role in the local ecosystems, contributing to decomposition and nutrient cycling.

Environments

Beaches

The beaches in Cape May serve as popular recreational destinations for people, aswell as provide dunes and coastal vegetation which give more organisms habitats and protects the land from flooding. Cities that have access to these beaches include Ocean City, Wildwood, Avalon, Cape May, and Sea Isle City

Woodlands

Cape May County's woodlands offer a diverse landscape with oak and pine trees, creating habitats for various wildlife. These inland areas contribute to the county's ecological diversity, providing both residents and visitors with serene natural settings and opportunities to learn about nature

Picture is from the Belleplain state forest

Wetlands

Cape May County features expansive wetlands. This vital ecosystem is characterized by salt marshes, estuaries, and waterlogged areas. These wetlands supports diverse plant life, marine life, athropodic life, avian life, and more. The wetlands play a crusial role in protecting habitats from flooding, erosion, and storm surges.
