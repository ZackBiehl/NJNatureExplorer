Atlantic County features a distinct environment comprising of charming beaches, rivers, wetlands, inlets and a slight influence of the Pine Barrons. The ecosystem hosts a variety of wildlife, contributing to the fun and interesting appeal.
Atlantic County's environment and ecosystems have common characteristics with Cape May County, Cumberland County, Burlington County, Ocean County, Gloucester County, and Camden County.

Climate

Atlantic County, along the Atlantic Ocean, has a Humid Subtropical Climate. With temperatures ranging from about 86°F in July to about 41°F in january. Nor'eastors, which are strong winds from the north east, are common during the winter and fall and can bring heavy rain, very strong winds, flooding and damage.
The Atlantic Hurricane season typically runs from June 1st to November 30th. However Hurricanes are infrequent in New Jersey as only 15 hurricanes have passed New Jersey since 1850 and few have made landfall. Usually turning into tropical storms before landfall. However, can be devasting for the coast due to potential Storm Surges that can form from the winds moving cyclonically around a storm. Storm surges cause water to overflow onto coastal lands and cause flooding in the area and can result in massive damage, injuries and even deaths

Wildlife

Atlantic County's mix of both coastal and inland habitats make the county a home for a variety of wildlife.

Mammals

Atlantic County is home to a variety of mammals. Common mammals include white-tailed deer, red foxes, groundhogs, eastern chipmunks, and various small rodents. Additionally, raccoons, skunks, and opossums are frequently found in the county's woodlands and suburban areas, contributing to the region's terrestrial biodiversity.

This section excludes marine mammals which can be found in the aquatic and aquatic marine life section.

Birds

Atlantic County has a diverse range of land-based birds that contribute to the local avian diversity. Common species include songbirds like robins, cardinals, and sparrows, as well as raptors such as hawks and owls. Wetland and lake areas attract waterfowl like ducks and herons, while shorebirds can be found along coastal zones. Atlantic County is a great spot for bird watching and has areas to watch at birds up close.

Aquatic and Marine Life

Atlantic County boasts an interesting coastal envrioment that is home to many different aquatic life. Common inhabitants iclude the Dolphins, Flounder, Striped Bass and various shellfish. The region also sees certain whales like Humpback and Minke whales, passing through during their migration patterns.These marine species contribute to the county's aquatic richness, making it an appealing destination for marine wildlife enthusiasts.

Insects and Arachnids

Atlantic County is home to incredibly diverse insects and arachnids. Butterflies, mantises, bees, beetles, dragonflies, moths, many different spiders and more. Common insects and arachnids found in the area include the common Eastern Bumblebees, Polyphemus Moth, Green June Beetles, Daddy Long Leg Spiders, Yellow Garden Spiders and more.

Amphibians and Reptiles

Atlantic County has a rich variety of amphibians and reptiles. Atlantic County's woodlands, wetlands, and suburban areas are home to many different frogs, toads, salamanders, newts, snakes, tutrles, and lizards. Common amphibians and reptiles include green frogs, box turtles, watersnakes, fowler's toads, and more.

Plants and Fungi

Flora

Atlantic County hosts a diverse range of flora, including native plants like beach heather and pitch pine in coastal areas, while inland regions feature hardwood forests with oak and maple trees. The county's varied landscapes support a rich tapestry of plant life, contributing to its ecological diversity.

Environments

Beaches

The beaches in Atlantic County serve as popular recreational destinations for people, aswell as provide dunes and coastal vegetation which give more organisms habitats and protects the land from flooding. Cities that have access to these beaches include Ventor, Margate, Atlantic city, Somers Point, Longport, Egg Harbor City, and Brigantine

Picture is of Atlantic City Beach

Woodlands

Atlantic County woodlands offer a rich selection of plant and animal life including oak and maple trees, diverse vegetation, and various wildlife species. The woodlands provide recreational areas for people to learn about nature and the environment. The woodlands can be found in parks, wildlife reserves and forests. These woodlands provide habitats for numerous organisms and contribute to the county's overall ecological diversity.

Both pictures are of Birch Grove Park

Wetlands

Atlantic County features expansive wetlands. This vital ecosystem is characterized by salt marshes, estuaries, and waterlogged areas. These wetlands supports diverse plant life, marine life, athropodic life, avian life, and more. The wetlands play a crusial role in protecting habitats from flooding, erosion, and storm surges.

Picture is of the wetlands in the Edwin B. Forsythe National Wildlife Refuge

Rivers, Creeks and Lakes

The Egg Harbor River that intersects Atlantic County contributes to the regions scenic beauty and provides habitats for diverse aquatic life aswell as waterfowl and aphibian and reptilian life. The river also provides recreation for the residents and any visitors. Aswell as various lakes throughout the county enhance the scenery and provide habitats for more organisms.
