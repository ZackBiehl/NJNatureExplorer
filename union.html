Union County has diverse ecosystems encompassing woodlands, the coast, and waterways, fostering a rich array of flora and fauna amidst a suburban landscape.

Union County's environment and ecosystems have common characteristics with Somerset County, Essex County, Monmouth County, and Mercer County.

Climate

Union County has a Humid Continental Climate in the west and a Humid Subropical Climate in the east. With temperatures ranging from about 86°F in July to about 24°F in january. Nor'eastors, which are strong winds from the north east, are common during the winter and fall and can bring heavy rain and very strong winds.
The Atlantic Hurricane season typically runs from June 1st to November 30th. However Hurricanes are infrequent in New Jersey as only 15 hurricanes have passed New Jersey since 1850 and few have made landfall. Usually turning into tropical storms before landfall. However, this can be devasting for the coast due to potential Storm Surges that can form from the winds moving cyclonically around a storm. Storm surges cause water to overflow onto coastal lands and cause flooding in the area and can result in massive damage, injuries and even deaths.

Wildlife

Union County holds diverse wildlife that thrives within its diverse ecosystems, from the coastal areas to the woodlands wildlife find a welcoming habitat.

Mammals

Union County boasts a diverse array of mammals that contribute to the region's ecological richness. These mammals, adapted to various habitats, include species such as foxes, skunks, raccoons, and rabbits. Their presence spans wooded areas, water sources, and grassy landscapes, creating a harmonious blend of urban and natural environments.

This section excludes marine mammals which can be found in the aquatic and marine life section.

Birds

Union County is home to a diverse avian population that spans various habitats, including urban, woodland, and coastal landscapes. The county's birds contribute to its ecological balance, with species adapted to both human-altered environments and more natural settings. Common birds include the American Robin, Northern Cardinal, and European Starling. This makes Union County a great place for bird watching all around.

Pictures from left to right starting at top left
American Redstart, Tufted Titmouse, Song Sparrow, Cackling Goose

Aquatic and Marine Life

Union County boasts a mix of freshwater and marine environments, supporting diverse aquatic life. Local ponds, lakes and rivers like Echo lake, host common freshwater fish like bass and sunfish. The nearby bay introduces marine elements, with opportunities to observe marine life such as blue crabs, flounder and even a few rare harbor seals. This aquatic diversity enhances the ecological richness of Union County, providing residents with a connection to both freshwater and marine ecosystems.

Insects and Arachnids

Union County is home to a variety of insects and arachnids, contributing to the local biodiversity. Butterflies such as the Eastern Tiger Swallowtail and bees like the Honeybee play essential roles in pollination, enhancing the health of plant communities. Ants, beetles, and spiders, including species like the Eastern Carpenter Ant and the Garden Spider, are commonly found in urban and natural environments, showcasing their adaptability. The county's green spaces, including parks and gardens, provide habitats for this diverse range of insect and arachnid species.

Amphibians and Reptiles

Union County is home to a variety of amphibians and reptiles, enriching the local biodiversity. In aquatic areas like ponds, rivers and lakes, you can find amphibians like the Northern Green Frog and Eastern Newt. Reptiles, including the Eastern Painted Turtle and Northern Black Racer snake, inhabit diverse environments from parks to natural habitats. These species play crucial roles in the local ecosystem, contributing to insect control and maintaining a balance in the food chain.

Plants and Fungi

Flora

Union County boasts diverse flora, enhancing the local environment. Native trees like the Eastern Redbud and Tulip Tree grace parks and natural areas, offering shade and visual appeal. Wildflowers such as the Black-Eyed Susan and New York Aster add vibrant splashes of color to open spaces.

Fungi

Union County harbors a diverse range of fungi that plays crucial roles in the local ecosystem. In wooded areas and natural spaces, mushrooms like Amanita species and the Fly Agaric can be found. These fungi contribute to decomposition, recycling nutrients back into the soil.

Environments

Rivers, Creeks and lakes

Union County features a network of rivers, creeks, and lakes that enhance its landscape. The Rahway River flows through scenic areas, offering recreational opportunities and habitat for organisms. Various creeks weave through urban and natural spaces. Lakes, like those in Echo Lake Park and Warinanco Park, provide homes for all types of organisms and recreational activities for residents.

Woodlands

Union County's woodlands provide habitats for a wide array of organims from small rodents to big bears. These wooded areas offer habitat for wildlife and support various plant species, as well as provide areas to residents to connect, experience and learn about nature. Notable woodlands in the area include Watchung Reservation, Lenape Park, and Ash Brook Reservation.

Parks and Wildlife Reserves

List of Parks and Reserves to Check Out

  • Unami Park - Great for a picnic and has various sports fields and courts!
  • Watchung Reservation - Over 2,000 acres preserve with over 50 miles of trails!
  • Lenape Park - Basketball, Pickleball, and tennis courts along with a peaceful, paved path along the river, perfect for a jog!
  • Ash Brook Reservation - A nature trail that leads through the historic swamp!
  • Echo Lake Park - A short and very enjoyable trail around Echo Lake, a great spot for a scenic pedal boat ride!
  • Nomahegan Park - Trails around and through the park along with 2 baseball fields, a soccer field and picnic areas!
  • Washington Park - Shared with Hudson County, has multiple sports courts and fields, a playground, and over 3.5 miles of trails!
