Burlington County's environment and ecosystems have common characteristics with Ocean County, Atlantic County, Camden County, Monmouth County, and Mercer County

Climate

Burlington County has a Humid Subtropical Climate. With temperatures ranging from about 87°F in July to about 22°F in january. While Burlington County has been struck with hurricanes occasionally it is not common. Severe thunderstorms are common during the warm season. During the colder seasons Burlington gets an average snowfall of about 18-22 inches. In the Pine Barrens it is not uncommon for radiative cooling to occur which is the process by which a body loses heat by thermal radiation. Due to sandy soil, on clear and dry nights these areas might be 10 to 15 °F colder than the surrounding areas

Wildlife

Burlington County's wildlife is characterized by a diverse array of species adapted to its pine barrons, wetlands, and suburban areas.

Mammals

Burlington County hosts a variety of mammals in its diverse landscapes. Common sightings include white-tailed deer, foxes, raccoons, and eastern cottontail rabbits. Woodlands, wetlands, and suburban areas provide habitats for these mammals, contributing to the county's terrestrial biodiversity.

American Black Bear, White-tailed Deer, Northern Short-Tailed Shrew, Star-Nosed Mole

Birds

Burlington County is a haven for birdwatchers, offering a diverse range of avian life. Common sightings include songbirds, raptors, waterfowl, and migratory species. The county's woodlands, wetlands, and rivers provide vital habitats for resident and migratory birds, making it a vibrant destination for bird enthusiasts.

Pictures from left to right starting at top left
Bald Eagle, Red-winged Blackbird, Great Crested Flycatcher, Downy Woodpecker

Aquatic and Marine Life

Burlington County, situated inland, does not have direct access to sea environments. As a landlocked county in New Jersey, its aquatic ecosystems primarily consist of rivers, streams, and freshwater bodies, which support various freshwater fish and invertebrates such as catfish, pickerel, white perch and more.

Insects and Arachnids

Common sightings of insects and arachnids in Burlington County include Red-Spotted Admiral, Monarchs, various bees and beetles, the Eastern Comma, the Carolina Mantis, and more. The county's varied landscapes, including woodlands and suburban areas, offer habitats for a rich insect and arachnid biodiversity.

Pictures from left to right starting at top left
Eastern Carpenter Bee, Southern Black Widow, Imperial Moth, Lone Star Tick

Amphibians and Reptiles

In Burlington County, the varied ecosystems, including wetlands and woodlands, support a rich array of amphibians and reptiles. Common amphibians include Eastern American toads, spring peepers, and red-backed salamanders. Reptile sightings may include Eastern box turtles, garter snakes, and the Northern water snake. These habitats are essential environments for many amphibians and reptiles.

Plants and Fungi

Flora

Burlington County has a diverse flora with notable features such as oak and pine woodlands, wetland vegetation like cattails and reeds, and lush blueberry bushes. The county's landscapes showcase a variety of plant life, contributing to its ecological richness and agricultural significance. Hammonton is especially famous for their blueberries!

Fungi

Burlington County supports a variety of fungi, including mushrooms and molds. These organisms play a vital role in the local ecosystems, contributing to decomposition, nutrient cycling, and the overall health of woodlands and other natural environments.

Environments

Woodlands

Burlington County's woodlands offer a diverse landscape featuring oak and pine trees, creating habitats for various wildlife. These woodlands, including areas like the Pine Barrens, contribute to the county's ecological richness, providing both residents and visitors with serene natural settings and opportunities for outdoor recreation.

Rivers, Creels and Lakes

Burlington County is traversed by several rivers, including the Rancocas Creek and the Delaware River, the county is also spotted with various lakes, including the beautiful Medford Lakes. These waterways contribute to the county's diverse ecosystems, supporting aquatic life and providing recreational opportunities for residents. The rivers and lakes play a crucial role in shaping the landscape and enriching the natural environment of Burlington County.

Wetlands

Burlington County's wetlands play a vital role in supporting diverse flora and fauna. These wetland areas contribute to the county's ecological health by providing critical habitats, assisting in flood control, and maintaining water quality.
