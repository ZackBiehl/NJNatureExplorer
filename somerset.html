Somerset County

Somerset County offers a diverse environment with woodlands, suburbs, preserved natural areas, and freshwater aquatic areas.

Somerset County's environment and ecosystems have common characteristics with Morris County, Union County, Middlesex County, Hunterdon County, and Mercer County.

Climate

Somerset County has a Humid Continental Climate. With temperatures ranging from about 85°F in July to about 18°F in january. Nor'eastors, which are strong winds from the north east, are common during the winter and fall and can bring heavy rain and very strong winds.
The Atlantic Hurricane season typically runs from June 1st to November 30th. However Hurricanes are infrequent in New Jersey as only 15 hurricanes have passed New Jersey since 1850 and few have made landfall. Usually turning into tropical storms before landfall. Since Somerset County is inland it is extremely rare for hurricanes or tropical storms to ever make it to the county.

Wildlife

Somerset County is characterized by a harmonious blend of suburban communities, agricultural landscapes, and preserved natural areas, fostering a diverse ecosystem

Mammals

Somerset County is home to various mammals in its diverse environments. Typical species include red foxes, eastern chipmunks, groundhogs, and rare sightings of bobcats and various smaller rodents. These species are able to adapt to the mix of urban, woodlands, and river/lake environments.

Birds

Somerset County boasts a diverse avian population thriving in its varied landscapes. Common species include blue jays, house sparrows, and mourning doves, adapting to both urban and suburban environments. The county's avian spectrum also encompasses more specialized species like red-tailed hawks, contributing to the vibrant birdlife in Somerset County.

Common Merganser, Green heron, Mourning Dove, Tree swallow

Aquatic and Marine Life

Somerset County, located inland in New Jersey, primarily supports freshwater life within its diverse aquatic ecosystems. Local rivers and streams are inhabited by species such as bass, sunfish, and catfish, contributing to the freshwater biodiversity.

Insects and Arachnids

Somerset County hosts a variety of insects and arachnids across its diverse environments. Common arachnids include orb-weaving spiders and wolf spiders, while insects like honeybees, butterflies such as the Eastern Tiger Swallowtail, and ants thrive in both urban and natural habitats. More specialized species like dragonflies and beetles further enrich the insect and arachnid diversity.

Amphibians and Reptiles

Somerset County provides habitats for a range of amphibians and reptiles across its diverse landscapes. Common species include green frogs, painted turtles, eastern red-backed salamander, and various frogs and snakes. These species are adapted to the river, woodland, and urban areas.

Environments

Rivers, Creeks and lakes

Somerset County is intersected by a network of rivers, creeks, and lakes that enhance its natural allure. The Raritan River and various creeks flow through the county, providing a vital watercours. Lakes like Lake Somerset offer recreational spaces and habitats for aquatic life.These water bodies play a crucial role in shaping Somerset County's landscapes, providing both natural beauty and opportunities for outdoor enjoyment

Woodlands

Somerset County is adorned with captivating woodlands that invite outdoor enthusiasts to explore their scenic beauty. With locations like Washington Valley Park, Duke Farms, and the Sourland Mountain Preserve, the county offers residents and visitors serene spaces for activities like hiking and nature walks.
